Compass is built for one type of person: someone who can build a working app with AI tools but is figuring out the business side for the first time.

It's not generic startup advice. It's not written for funded teams with engineers. It's written for the solo founder or small team using Lovable, Base44, Bolt, or Cursor, who shipped something, has users, and now needs to know what to do next.

## The seven manuals

| Manual | What it covers |
| --- | --- |
| **Foundation** | What actually changed, who this is for, and what it takes before you touch a tool |
| **Shape** | Define the problem, the customer, and whether it's worth building |
| **Build** | Go from spec to a live, working product — web or mobile |
| **Launch** | Get it in front of real people, measure what happens, iterate fast |
| **Monetize** | Start charging, convert free users, make revenue repeatable |
| **Grow** | Build the channels that bring in more of the right users and keep them longer |
| **Operate** | Build the systems that keep the business running as it scales |

Each manual is made up of chapters. Each chapter has one job: give you what you need to complete that step, then move on.

## You don't have to read it in order

If you already have a product and you're stuck on converting free users to paid, start at Monetize. If you're trying to figure out whether your idea is worth building, start at Shape. If your product is running but the operational layer is falling apart, jump to Operate.

<FieldNote title="One exception">
If you're building something for the first time, read Foundation and Shape before you open a builder or write a line of code. The decisions you make before you start building determine more about your outcome than any feature you'll ship later.
</FieldNote>

## How each chapter is structured

Every chapter follows the same format:

- A clear goal: what you walk away able to do
- Context: why this matters and what most people get wrong (~30% of the chapter)
- How to do it: step by step, specific, actionable (~70%)
- A checklist: the things to have done before you move on
- A prompt: something you can run through an AI tool to speed up the work

## Where Mantle fits in

Mantle is a CRM and billing platform built specifically for app builders. Where relevant, chapters reference Mantle features — usage events, Flows, segments, hosted billing — that make specific operational steps significantly faster.

These are always called out clearly and always optional. You don't need Mantle to follow any of the guidance here. But if you want the operational layer handled without stitching together five separate tools, it's worth knowing what's available.
