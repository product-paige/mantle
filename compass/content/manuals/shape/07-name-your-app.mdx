The perfect name doesn't exist. A good one is available, easy to say, and doesn't confuse people. Everything else is a rabbit hole.

Here's the reality: your name is not your brand at this stage. Your brand is what people say after they've used the thing. The name is just how they find it. Slack was called Linefeed. Notion launched as Notional. Neither name killed the product. Spending three weeks in a naming spiral is not the move. Pick something good enough and ship.

## What makes a name work

| Works | Doesn't work |
|---|---|
| Easy to say and spell after hearing it once | Requires explanation |
| Not tied to one feature or market | Describes exactly one use case |
| Available as a .com or close variant | Every domain variation is taken |
| No major trademark conflicts | Owned by someone with a legal budget |
| No bad associations in other languages | A slur in Portuguese |

**What's working in app naming right now:**
- Everyday objects — Stripe. Loom. Clay. Bolt. Familiar, room to grow, common words are more likely to have acquirable domains.
- Single syllable — Arc. Cal. Warp. Short, punchy, impossible to misspell.
- Invented words that sound real — Figma. Notion. Retool. Unique enough to own in search and trademark.

**What's not:**
- Frankenwords and deliberate misspellings — "Instasynk," "Cloudifyr," "Leadzr." 2014 energy.
- Dropped vowels — Tumblr-style had a moment. That moment passed.
- Descriptive compounds — "InvoiceTool," "LeadTracker." SEO-friendly but impossible to trademark and hard to grow beyond.

## The process

**Generate 20 options without filtering.** Nouns, objects, invented words, single syllables, two words combined — anything. Don't edit while you generate. You need range before you can narrow. Set a 20-minute timer.

**Cut anything that fails the criteria.** Can they say it correctly after hearing it once? Can they spell it? Does it describe exactly one feature? Is it a frankenword? Too close to a competitor? Cut it. You should be down to 5–8.

**Check availability.** For each remaining name: domain (.com first, then `get[name].com` or `[name]app.com`), trademark via USPTO, social handles, app stores if relevant. Buy the domain the same day you pick the name.

**Check for bad associations.** Five minutes now saves a painful rebrand later. Run your shortlist through the language check prompt below if you're building for a global market.

**Test the top three with real people.** Show them the name — just the word, no context. Ask how they'd say it, how they'd spell it, what it makes them think of. Pick the one with the fewest confused reactions. Not the most excitement — the fewest confused reactions.

## If you're building a mobile app

Mobile naming has a few extra considerations:

**App Store search optimization.** Your app name is a ranking factor in App Store and Google Play search. Including a relevant keyword after your brand name (e.g., "Loom — Video Messaging") helps with discoverability. Apple allows up to 30 characters for the name field — keep it short and leave room.

**Subtitle field.** Both stores give you a subtitle field (30 characters) that's also indexed for search. This is where you put your one-liner keyword phrase, not your brand story. Think "Invoice Tracker for Freelancers" not "The Future of Invoicing."

**Check the stores before you commit.** Search your exact name in both App Store and Google Play before you register it anywhere. A name collision with an established app in your category is a problem that doesn't show up in a trademark search.

## Checklist

- [ ] I've generated at least 20 candidates without filtering
- [ ] I've cut anything that fails the say-and-spell test
- [ ] I've cut anything that boxes me into one feature or market
- [ ] I've checked domain, trademark, and handles for the top 3
- [ ] I've run a language check on global-market candidates
- [ ] I've tested the top 3 with real people who match my customer profile
- [ ] I've picked the one with the fewest confused reactions
- [ ] I bought the domain the same day

## Prompts

**Rate my name:**
> *Rate this product name on a scale of 1–10 and be brutally honest:*
> *Product name: [YOUR NAME]*
> *Product type: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]*
>
> *Evaluate: pronounceability, spellability, memorability, confusion risk, future-proofing. Give me a single overall score and list any deal-breaker issues. Don't be nice — I need to know if this name is a problem before I commit to it.*

**Check if it's taken:**
> *I'm considering the name [YOUR NAME] for my [PRODUCT TYPE]. Are there any well-known apps, SaaS products, or companies already using this name or something close? Flag anything that would cause confusion in search or with customers.*

**Language check:**
> *I'm considering the name [YOUR NAME] for a product targeting international markets. Does this word mean anything negative, inappropriate, or embarrassing in Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Mandarin, Japanese, Hindi, or Arabic? Does it sound phonetically similar to anything offensive? Be direct.*
