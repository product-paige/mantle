It's incredibly easy to skip planning in the AI era and jump straight into building. Guilty as charged. An idea pops into my head, I open Lovable, write a two-sentence prompt, and suddenly there's a working dashboard.

But if you want to build a real business, taking a beat upfront will save you weeks of wasted work later (and a ton of credits). Otherwise, you risk spending six weeks building something nobody wanted before abandoning it for the next big idea.

You still need clarity on what you're building, who it's for, and whether anyone actually wants it before you touch a tool.

Most solo founders and developers I work with have zero product marketing. Not because they think they don't need it, but because they don't know where to start or don't have the time. That's exactly why I wrote the Shape manual.

Think of this manual as the early strategy work most founders skip. It's practical whether you're using Lovable, Cursor, or writing every line yourself.

## What's inside

| Section | Summary |
| --- | --- |
| **1.1 Define the problem** | Before you build anything, get painfully clear on the problem. Who has it, when does it happen, and why does it matter enough to solve? |
| **1.2 Find your customer** | "Small businesses" is not a customer. Get specific enough that you could DM three real people who would actually use this. |
| **1.3 Map the competition** | Find what already exists, read the angry reviews, and look for the gaps. Competition is usually proof there's demand. |
| **1.4 Choose your platform** | Web app, mobile app, Shopify app, browser extension? Your platform changes your distribution, pricing, UX, and even your name. |
| **1.5 Map the workflow** | What's the simplest possible path from problem to value? Define the happy path before you start stacking features. |
| **1.6 Set your constraints** | Decide what you're *not* building early. Constraints prevent scope creep and save solo founders from building a bloated mess. |
| **1.7 Will they pay?** | Why would someone pay for this instead of using AI, hiring a freelancer, or doing nothing at all? |
| **1.8 Should you build this?** | A quick viability filter before you sink months into an idea. Some ideas should stay Notes-app thoughts. |
| **1.9 Test demand** | Validate interest before you build. Landing pages, waitlists, DMs, content, communities — real signals, not guesses. |
| **1.10 Plan your distribution** | How are your first 10–50 users actually going to find this? "I'll post on X" is not a strategy. |
| **1.11 Brand basics** | Name, domain, colors, fonts, vibe, voice. Enough brand direction to make decisions without spiraling for three weeks. |
| **1.12 What's next** | Pull everything into one clear shape document you can actually build from. This becomes your roadmap, prompt foundation, and source of truth moving forward. |

## Takeaway

The goal of this manual isn't to slow you down. It's to stop you from building blindly. AI made execution faster, but clarity still matters. The founders who win aren't the ones prompting the most. They're the ones who understand the problem, the customer, and how to actually get people to care.
