# Build your first MVP

The point of an MVP is not to be impressive. The point is to be honest.

An MVP is the smallest, ugliest, most incomplete version of your idea that puts a real version of your value in front of a real user. Nothing more. The features you'd love to add, the polish you'd love to ship, the integrations you have in your head — none of it matters yet.

This manual is about learning to ship that first version on purpose, and to learn from it on purpose, before you spend three months building something nobody asked for.

---

## What an MVP is and is not

An MVP is:

- the **shortest path** between a user and the core value of your idea
- a way to **buy information** about whether your idea works, fast and cheap
- something you can change, throw out, or rebuild without it hurting

An MVP is **not**:

- a finished product with the polish stripped off
- a beta of the thing you're already certain about
- an excuse to ship something half-broken and call it strategy

> If your MVP looks like a smaller version of your roadmap, you're building a roadmap, not an MVP.

---

## The MVP mindset

The hardest part is restraint. You'll feel constant pressure — internal, mostly — to add the next thing, and the thing after that. The discipline is to ship before you're ready, and then to actually listen to what comes back.

A good MVP feels uncomfortable. It feels too small, too rough, too narrow. That's how you know it's the MVP and not the product.

---

## What this manual will help you do

This manual is built around the moves that get a real MVP out the door without trapping you in scope.

You'll learn how to:

- write a one-paragraph definition of your MVP that you can defend
- pick the *single* user and the *single* problem worth shipping for
- choose the smallest workflow that proves your value
- decide what to fake, what to manual, and what to actually build
- pick a stack you can ship in a weekend and survive in a quarter
- get your first 10 real users without "going viral"
- read the signal in their behavior, not just their compliments

---

## This manual is for you if

- you have an idea you can describe in a sentence but can't ship in a month
- you keep adding features instead of shipping the one
- you're stuck on tech choices that don't matter yet
- you can build, but you're not sure what to build first
- your "MVP" already has a roadmap, a brand, and a pricing page

---

## What success looks like

By the end of this manual, you should have:

- a written one-paragraph MVP definition
- a short list of features you're explicitly *not* building
- a clear definition of "value delivered" you can test against
- a plan to get your first real users in their hands inside two weeks
- a lightweight way to capture what you learn from those first users

If those are in place, the next 90 days are about iterating on signal — not arguing about scope.
